Client Reporting

Designing a reporting system to close a market gap

Before this project, the platform did not provide a built-in way for teams to generate client reports. Users exported data and manually assembled reports using external tools, creating fragmented workflows and increasing the time required to prepare client updates.

Competitors had already introduced integrated reporting capabilities, making this a key gap in the product offering.

My role focused on researching reporting workflows and designing a scalable reporting experience that could deliver immediate value while working within technical constraints.

The Challenge

How can we introduce client reporting into an existing platform in a way that delivers immediate value while remaining scalable as the product evolves?

Reporting touches multiple parts of a product ecosystem — data pipelines, visualisation, user workflows, and sharing capabilities. Designing the experience required balancing user expectations with the technical limitations of the underlying data infrastructure.

The goal was not only to design the reporting interface, but also to help define how the feature should evolve over time.

Constraints

  • Existing data architecture — Reporting relied on complex data systems that limited which metrics could initially be surfaced.
  • Technical prioritisation — Engineering resources required the feature to be delivered iteratively rather than as a single release.
  • Varied reporting needs — Different teams required different reporting formats, making flexibility an important consideration.

Research

Discovery

Research began with an analysis of existing customer feedback, internal requests, and product discussions related to reporting. These sources highlighted consistent friction around how teams prepared client updates.

Users frequently exported raw data and assembled reports manually in spreadsheets or presentation software.

Key Insights

  • Reporting workflows were fragmented. Users relied on multiple tools to compile information before sharing insights with clients.
  • Consistency was difficult. Teams created reports manually, leading to inconsistent formats and messaging.
  • Clear visualisation mattered. Users needed visual summaries that communicated performance quickly.
  • Reporting was a competitive expectation. Several competing platforms already offered integrated reporting tools.

Product Insight

One important discovery was that reporting requirements varied significantly between teams. While some users required detailed analytics, others primarily needed clear summaries for client communication.

This insight reinforced the importance of starting with a focused set of high-value reporting features and expanding capabilities over time.

Design

Planning an iterative release

Because reporting relied heavily on underlying data infrastructure, feature development required close collaboration with engineering teams.

Through backlog refinement sessions, we prioritised reporting capabilities based on both user value and technical feasibility.

This process helped define a phased release strategy that allowed the product to introduce reporting quickly while expanding functionality in later iterations.

Design exploration

Several interface concepts were explored to determine how reporting should fit within the existing platform.

Design comparisons focused on discoverability, report creation workflows, and the clarity of data presentation.

The final direction prioritised simple report generation, clear visual summaries, and reusable report structures that teams could use consistently across clients.

Balancing flexibility and clarity

Rather than designing an overly complex reporting system, the initial release focused on delivering clear and reliable summaries of the most important metrics.

This approach allowed users to quickly generate client-ready insights while establishing a strong foundation for future reporting capabilities.

Outcomes

The introduction of client reporting closed an important competitive gap and significantly improved the platform’s value for teams communicating results to clients.

  • Reporting capabilities allowed users to generate client insights directly within the platform.
  • Manual reporting workflows were reduced as teams began using integrated reports.
  • Adoption of the reporting feature grew by 250% within the first year.
  • The iterative release strategy allowed reporting functionality to expand while continuing to support new use cases.

This project demonstrated how aligning research, product strategy, and engineering constraints can deliver meaningful user value while supporting long-term platform growth.
